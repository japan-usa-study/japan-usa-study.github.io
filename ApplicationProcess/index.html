Application Process

Your complete guide to applying for US graduate programs

The application process for studying in the United States can be overwhelming, but we're here to break it down step by step. Follow this guide to make the process smoother and more manageable.

1Research and Shortlist Universities

  • Identify universities that match your academic and personal goals.
  • Check application deadlines and specific requirements for each university.
  • Use resources like EducationUSA and U.S. News Rankings to compare options.

2Prepare Application Documents

  • Transcripts: Collect official transcripts from your previous institutions.
  • Test Scores: Take standardized tests like TOEFL/IELTS, SAT, GRE, or GMAT, depending on program requirements.
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP): Write a compelling essay about your academic goals and why you're applying.
  • Letters of Recommendation: Request references from professors or professionals who know your work.
  • Resume/CV: Highlight your academic, extracurricular, and professional achievements.

3Submit Applications

  • Create an account on the university's application portal
  • Pay attention to deadlines and ensure all documents are uploaded correctly.
  • Pay the application fee (if applicable).

4Apply for Scholarships and Financial Aid

  • Research scholarships offered by universities and external organizations.
  • Look for funding options through JASSO for Japanese students.

5Await Results

  • Most universities notify applicants via email or their portal. Keep an eye on deadlines for responding to offers.
  • Start preparing for your visa application if you receive an acceptance letter.

6Secure Your Student Visa

  • Obtain your I-20 form from the university to apply for an F-1 visa.
  • Pay the SEVIS fee and schedule a visa interview at the U.S. embassy or consulate in Japan.
  • Attend the interview with all required documents, including proof of funding and acceptance letters.

7Plan Your Move

  • Arrange housing, whether on-campus or off-campus.
  • Book your flights and prepare essential documents for travel.
  • Attend orientation programs to settle into your new academic life.

Questions? Please check back soon for contact options. We're here to help you navigate the application process and achieve your dream of studying in the U.S.
